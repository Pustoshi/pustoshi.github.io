Tubes

Subway
navigation app.

Problem:
We thought that our app might be useful for tourists.Because they mostly use subway and usually live in a hotel in the central areas and travel within center of the city. SL has big functionality, which is more convenient for people who already live in Stockholm, SL doesn’t show subway map, but contains information about suburb trains and buses. So we wanted to create app which does only 1 function, very simple and minimalistic.

User Portrait
Let's look at Nina

She is a russian girl who came to Stockholm for a week. She stays in a hotel which is located in the center of the city. She wants to explore main attractions of the city. Nina wants to use subway if needed. Also she doesn’t have any internet connection, because she has a russian sim-card.

Goals

She wants to explore city fast because she has limited time. And she wants to be able to use navigation app without internet.

Frustrations

Nina doesn’t want to stop in a crowded subway to read and translate signs. She doesn’t speak english. She wants app to tell her where she should enter to go off in a right position to change the line or to find the right exit. Also Nina’s phone is overloaded with many different apps. And she wants to use 1 app for every city in the world.

User Scenario

Nina is a traveler, she likes discovering new places so she wants to find a universal app for all big and popular cities in the world. Nina wants to optimize her time and she doesn’t like finding new apps for every city she visits.

User Flow

User flow helped to understand the core and essence of the app. And remove redundut functionality.

Sketching and Wireframing

After working on many different sketches I chose the path I wannted to stick with and created wireframes and interactive prototype in Figma.
